# -Data-analysis-module
Statistical analysis of clinical and biological datasets using SPSS.
# Overview:
This module involved applying statistical methods in SPSS to multiple real-world clinical datasets to answer biological and health research questions, covering the analysis of gene expression data comparing Huntington's Disease (HD) patients against a control group. Part 1 of the assessment focused on identifying significantly dysregulated genes across 10 genes, including THBS3, DDR1, CCNB1, and SMAD7. while the other parts also covered newborn growth, neonatal surgical recovery, infant weight by birth order, diabetes treatment outcomes, and the association between herbicides and cancer in dogs. 
# What Was Done
Descriptive statistics and independent samples t-tests on newborn birth measurements (weight, length, head circumference by gender)
ANOVA and Pearson correlation on neonatal surgical length-of-stay data
One-way ANOVA, two-way ANOVA, post hoc (Tukey) tests, and ANCOVA on infant weight by parity and gender
Chi-square tests and binary logistic regression comparing diabetes treatments (Metformin vs Metformin+Rosiglitazone vs Lifestyle) in teenagers
Chi-square and odds ratio analysis examining 2,4-D herbicide exposure and cancer risk in dogs

# Tools & Techniques
SPSS · Descriptive Statistics · t-tests · ANOVA · ANCOVA · Chi-Square · Logistic Regression · Pearson Correlation · Data Visualisation
